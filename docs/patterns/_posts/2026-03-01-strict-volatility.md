description: |
  A term suggested by Noam Elkies in August 1998 for
  the proportion of cells involved in a period n [oscillator](/patterns/oscillator) which
  themselves oscillate with period n.  For prime n this is the same as
  the ordinary [volatility](/patterns/volatility).  Periods with known strictly-volatile
  oscillators include 1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 8, 13, 15, 22, 30, 33, and 177.
  Examples include [figure-8](/patterns/figure-8), [Kok's galaxy](/patterns/kok-s-galaxy), [smiley](/patterns/smiley), and
  [pentadecathlon](/patterns/pentadecathlon).  A composite example is the following p22, found by
  Nicolay Beluchenko on 4 March 2009.
