description: |
  The only other two-[glider](/patterns/glider) collision besides the
  standard [kickback](/patterns/kickback) that produces a clean output glider with no
  leftover [ash](/patterns/ash).  The 180-degree change in direction is occasionally
  useful in [glider synthesis](/patterns/glider-synthesis), but is rarely used in [signal](/patterns/signal)
  circuitry or in [self-supporting](/patterns/self-supporting) patterns like the [Caterpillar](/patterns/caterpillar) or
  [Centipede](/patterns/centipede), because 90-degree collisions are generally much easier
  to arrange.
