description: |
  Any [oscillator](/patterns/oscillator) whose [rotor](/patterns/rotor) consists of a closed
  chain of cells each of which is adjacent to exactly two other rotor
  cells.  Compare [babbling brook](/patterns/babbling-brook).  Examples include the [bipole](/patterns/bipole), the
  [blinker](/patterns/blinker), the [clock](/patterns/clock), the [cuphook](/patterns/cuphook), the [Gray counter](/patterns/gray-counter), the
  [quad](/patterns/quad), the [scrubber](/patterns/scrubber), the [skewed quad](/patterns/skewed-quad) and the p2 [snake pit](/patterns/snake-pit). The
  following diagram shows a p2 example (by Dean Hickerson, May 1993)
  with a larger rotor.  See [ring of fire](/patterns/ring-of-fire) for a very large one.
