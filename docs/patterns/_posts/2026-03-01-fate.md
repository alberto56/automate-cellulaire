description: |
  The result of evolving a pattern until its final behaviour is
  known.  This answers such questions such as whether or not the
  pattern remains finite, what its growth rate is, what [period](/patterns/period) the
  final state may settle into, and what its final [census](/patterns/census) is.  All
  small Life objects seem to eventually settle down into a mix of
  oscillators, simple spaceships, and occasionally small puffers.  See
  [methuselah](/patterns/methuselah), [soup](/patterns/soup), [ash](/patterns/ash).
  Most sufficiently large random patterns are expected to grow
  forever due to the production of [switch engine](/patterns/switch-engine)s at their boundary.
  Engineered Life objects - and therefore also sufficiently large and
  unlikely random patterns - can have more interesting behaviour, such
  as [breeder](/patterns/breeder)s, [sawtooth](/patterns/sawtooth)s, and prime calculators.  Some objects have
  even been constructed or designed having an [unknown fate](/patterns/unknown-fate).
