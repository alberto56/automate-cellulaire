description: |
  A computer program or script that automates the search
  for Life objects having certain desired properties.  These are used
  because the difficulty of finding previously unknown Life objects now
  commonly exceeds the patience, speed, and accuracy of humans.
  Various types of search programs are used for finding objects such as
  [spaceship](/patterns/spaceship)s, [oscillator](/patterns/oscillator)s, [drifter](/patterns/drifter)s, [catalyst](/patterns/catalyst)s, [soup](/patterns/soup)s,
  [Garden of Eden](/patterns/garden-of-eden)s, and [slow salvo](/patterns/slow-salvo)s.
  Some search programs generate [partial result](/patterns/partial-result)s as they are
  running, so even if they don't complete successfully, something of
  use might still be salvaged from the run.
  Example search programs are [dr](/patterns/dr), [lifesrc](/patterns/lifesrc), [gfind](/patterns/gfind), and
  [Bellman](/patterns/bellman).
  There are other types of programs which don't perform searches as
  such, but instead perform large constructions.  These are used to
  correctly complete very complicated objects such as the
  [Caterpillar](/patterns/caterpillar), [Gemini](/patterns/gemini), [Caterloopillar](/patterns/caterloopillar), and
  [universal constructor](/patterns/universal-constructor)-based spaceships such as the [Demonoid](/patterns/demonoid)s and
  [Orthogonoid](/patterns/orthogonoid)s.
