description: |
  A reaction performed by a [convoy](/patterns/convoy) of [spaceship](/patterns/spaceship)s (or
  other moving objects) which converts a common stationary object into
  a glider without harming the convoy. This provides one way for
  [signal](/patterns/signal)s that have been frozen in place by some previous reaction to
  be released for use.
  Simple reactions using period 4 c/2 spaceships have been found for
  reanimating a [block](/patterns/block), [boat](/patterns/boat), [beehive](/patterns/beehive), [ship](/patterns/ship), [loaf](/patterns/loaf), [bi-block](/patterns/bi-block),
  or [toad](/patterns/toad).  The most interesting of these is for a [beehive](/patterns/beehive) since it
  seems to require an unusual p4 spaceship.      Reanimation of a [loaf](/patterns/loaf) is used many times in the [Caterloopillar](/patterns/caterloopillar).
  It is also used in the [Caterpillar](/patterns/caterpillar) as part of its [catch and throw](/patterns/catch-and-throw)
  mechanism.  Finally, reanimation can produce [rake](/patterns/rake)s from some
  [puffer](/patterns/puffer)s.  See [stop and restart](/patterns/stop-and-restart) for a similar idea that applies to
  [Herschel conduit](/patterns/herschel-conduit)s and other [signal](/patterns/signal) [circuit](/patterns/circuit)ry.
  There are small objects which have no known reanimation reactions
  using c/2 ships other than the brute force method of hitting them
  with the output of [rake](/patterns/rake)s.
