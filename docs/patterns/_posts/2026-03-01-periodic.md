description: |
  For [circuit](/patterns/circuit) mechanisms, "periodic" is the opposite of [p1](/patterns/p1)
  or [stable](/patterns/stable).  Periodic [circuit](/patterns/circuit)s necessarily contain [oscillator](/patterns/oscillator)s,
  and therefore they can generally only accept input [signal](/patterns/signal)s that are
  [synchronized](/patterns/synchronized) to the combined [period](/patterns/period) of those oscillators (but see
  [universal regulator](/patterns/universal-regulator)).
  For [signal](/patterns/signal) [stream](/patterns/stream)s, "periodic" means that signals will only be
  present in the stream at one out of every n ticks, where n is the
  [period](/patterns/period) of the stream.  In a periodic [intermittent stream](/patterns/intermittent-stream) there
  may be gaps, so that signals do not always appear at every nth tick.
  However, if a signal does appear, its distance measured in ticks from
  previous and future signals will always be an exact multiple of n.
