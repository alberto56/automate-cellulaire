description: |
  The cells of an [oscillator](/patterns/oscillator) that are always on.  Compare
  [rotor](/patterns/rotor).  (The stator is sometimes taken to include also some of
  those cells which are always off.)  The stator is divided into the
  [bushing](/patterns/bushing) and the [casing](/patterns/casing).
  By analogy, the cells of an [eater](/patterns/eater) that remain on even when the
  eater is eating are considered to constitute the stator of the eater.
  This is not always well-defined, because an eater can have more than
  one eating action.
