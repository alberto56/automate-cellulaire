description: |
  A mechanism where a [Herschel](/patterns/herschel) or other
  active reaction completely destroys a [catalyst](/patterns/catalyst) in a particular
  location in a [conduit](/patterns/conduit).  After passing through or past that
  location, the same reaction then recreates the catalyst in exactly
  its original position.  This type of catalysis is surprisingly common
  in [signal](/patterns/signal) [circuit](/patterns/circuit)ry.  For an example, see
  [transparent block reaction](/patterns/transparent-block-reaction).
  The transparent object is most often a very common [still life](/patterns/still-life)
  such as a block or beehive.  Rarer objects are not unknown; for
  example, a transparent [loaf](/patterns/loaf) was found by Stephen Silver in October
  1997, in a very useful [elementary conduit](/patterns/elementary-conduit) making up part of a
  [Herschel receiver](/patterns/herschel-receiver).  However, not surprisingly, rarer objects are
  much less likely to reappear in exactly the correct location and
  orientation, so transparent reactions involving them are much more
  difficult to find, on average.
