    This eater can be constructed using a simple two-glider collision,
    as shown in [stamp collection](/patterns/stamp-collection).  It is often modified in various
    ways, or [weld](/patterns/weld)ed to other objects, to allow tighter packing of
    [circuit](/patterns/circuit)s or to allow a [signal](/patterns/signal) [stream](/patterns/stream) to pass close by.  See
    [clearance](/patterns/clearance) for an eater1 variant that is 1[hd](/patterns/hd) shorter to the
    southeast than the standard fishhook form.  An eater1 can also be
    used as a 90-degree [one-time](/patterns/one-time) [turner](/patterns/turner).
    Its ability to eat various objects was discovered by Bill Gosper in
    1971.  The fishhook eater can consume a glider, a [LWSS](/patterns/lwSs), and a
    [MWSS](/patterns/mwSs) as shown below.  It is not able to consume an [HWSS](/patterns/hwSs), however.
    See [honey bit](/patterns/honey-bit) or [killer toads](/patterns/killer-toads) for that.
