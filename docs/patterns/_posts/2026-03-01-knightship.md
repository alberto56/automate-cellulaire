description: |
  Any [spaceship](/patterns/spaceship) of type (2m,m)/n - that is, a spaceship of
  any speed that moves obliquely in a (2,1) direction.  The first
  Conway's Life knightship was a variant of Andrew Wade's [Gemini](/patterns/gemini)
  spaceship, constructed in May 2010.  The next was an even slower
  knightship based on the [half-bakery reaction](/patterns/half-bakery-reaction).
  A knightship must be asymmetric and its period must be at least 6.
  This is barely within the range of current [search program](/patterns/search-program)s, as
  proven by the discovery on March 6, 2018 of an [elementary](/patterns/elementary)
  knightship, [Sir Robin](/patterns/sir-robin), by Adam P. Goucher and Tomas Rokicki.
  By analogy with the corresponding fairy chess pieces, spaceships of
  types (3m,m)/n, (3m,2m)/n and (4m,m)/n would presumably be called
  camelships, zebraships and giraffeships, respectively.  Such
  spaceships do exist (see [universal constructor](/patterns/universal-constructor)) but small
  elementary versions are even more difficult to search for.  Any of
  these ship types could be constructed by trivially modifying a Gemini
  spaceship, or less trivially by reprogramming one of the more recent
  small [Geminoid](/patterns/geminoid) [construction arm](/patterns/construction-arm)s, but as of July 2018 a camelship
  Gemini is the only example that has been explicitly built.
  Alternatively, the term "knightship" is regularly used to refer to
  any [oblique](/patterns/oblique) spaceship, such as the original [Gemini](/patterns/gemini) or the
  [waterbear](/patterns/waterbear).
