description: |
  (p46)  Found by Bill Gosper in 1971, this was the
  basis of all known [true](/patterns/true) p46 [gun](/patterns/gun)s, and all known p46 oscillators
  except for [glider](/patterns/glider) [signal](/patterns/signal) loops using [Snark](/patterns/snark)s, until the
  discovery of [Tanner's p46](/patterns/tanner-s-p46) in 2017.  See [new gun](/patterns/new-gun) for an example.
  There are numerous ways to stabilize the ends, two of which are shown
  in the diagram.  On the left is David Bell's [double block reaction](/patterns/double-block-reaction)
  (which results in a shorter, but wider, shuttle than usual), and on
  the right is the stabilization by a single block.  This latter method
  produces the very large [twin bees shuttle spark](/patterns/twin-bees-shuttle-spark) which is useful in
  a number of ways.  See [metamorphosis](/patterns/metamorphosis) for an example.  Adding a
  symmetrically placed block below this one suppresses the spark.  See
  also [p54 shuttle](/patterns/p54-shuttle).
