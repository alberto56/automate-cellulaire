description: |
  Any small [stable](/patterns/stable) [signal](/patterns/signal) [conduit](/patterns/conduit) that produces one
  output glider for every two input gliders, with a 90 degree
  reflection.  These can act as period-doublers for any glider stream
  whose period is at least equal to their repeat time, and so adding
  one of these to a single glider [gun](/patterns/gun) often results in a pattern much
  smaller than the older [technology](/patterns/technology) of crossing the output of two
  guns.
  The available semi-Snarks differ in their complexity, size, repeat
  time, and the colour of their output gliders.  The [CC semi-Snark](/patterns/cc-semi-snark)
  was the first one found, and the term "semi-Snark" is often used
  specifically for this object.  The "CC" prefix stands for
  [colour-changing](/patterns/colour-changing), by contrast with the more recently discovered
  [colour-preserving](/patterns/colour-preserving) [CP semi-Snark](/patterns/cp-semi-snark).
  There are also CC and CP variants of a semi-Snark based on a
  two-[glider](/patterns/glider) to [century](/patterns/century) [converter](/patterns/converter) discovered by Tanner Jacobi in
  November 2017.  These [semi-cenark](/patterns/semi-cenark)s are the fastest semi-Snarks
  known as of July 2018, with a [repeat time](/patterns/repeat-time) as low as 50 ticks, or a
  periodic input rate as low as 36 ticks.
