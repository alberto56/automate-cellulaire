description: |
  A [composite conduit](/patterns/composite-conduit), one of the original sixteen
  [Herschel conduit](/patterns/herschel-conduit)s, discovered by Paul Callahan in October 1997.  It
  is made up of three [elementary conduit](/patterns/elementary-conduit)s, HF95P + PF35W + WFx46H.
  After 176 ticks, it produces an inverted [Herschel](/patterns/herschel) at (45, 0)
  relative to the input.  The [recovery time](/patterns/recovery-time) of the standard form
  shown here is 92 ticks, but see the [PF35W](/patterns/pf35w) entry for a variant
  discovered in November 2017 that lowers the repeat time to 73 ticks.
  A [ghost Herschel](/patterns/ghost-herschel) in the pattern below marks the output location.
