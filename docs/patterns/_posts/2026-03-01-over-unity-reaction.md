description: |
  An important concept in [gun](/patterns/gun) and
  [macro-spaceship](/patterns/macro-spaceship) construction.  To be a good candidate for building
  one of these types of patterns with a new period or speed, a
  stationary reaction (for a gun) or a moving reaction (for a
  macro-spaceship) must be able to produce some number of output
  [signal](/patterns/signal)s, strictly greater than the number of input signals required
  to maintain the reaction.  The extra signal becomes a gun's output
  [stream](/patterns/stream), or may be used in a variety of ways to construct the
  supporting [track](/patterns/track) for a macro-spaceship.  By implication,
  "over-unity" refers to the ratio of output signals to input signals.
  If all signal outputs must be used up to sustain a stationary
  reaction, a high-period [oscillator](/patterns/oscillator) may still be possible.  See
  [emu](/patterns/emu) for example.
