description: |
  A [breeder](/patterns/breeder) constructed by Nick Gotts in February 1997.  In the
  original version Jaws had an initial [population](/patterns/population) of 150, which at
  the time was the smallest for any known pattern with
  [superlinear growth](/patterns/superlinear-growth).  In November 1997 Gotts produced a 130-cell
  Jaws using some [switch engine](/patterns/switch-engine) [predecessor](/patterns/predecessor)s found by Paul
  Callahan.  See [switch-engine ping-pong](/patterns/switch-engine-ping-pong) for the lowest-population
  superlinear growth pattern as of July 2018, along with a list of the
  record-holders.
  Jaws consists of eight pairs of switch engines which produce a new
  block-laying switch engine (plus masses of junk) every 10752
  generations.  It is therefore an MMS breeder.
