description: |
  A 41-cell 187x39 [superlinear growth](/patterns/superlinear-growth) pattern found by
  Bill Gosper in March 2006, who named it in honour of Nick Gotts,
  discoverer of many other low-population superlinear patterns, such as
  [Jaws](/patterns/jaws), the [mosquito](/patterns/mosquito)es, [teeth](/patterns/teeth), [catacryst](/patterns/catacryst) and [metacatacryst](/patterns/metacatacryst).
  See [switch-engine ping-pong](/patterns/switch-engine-ping-pong) for the lowest-population
  [superlinear growth](/patterns/superlinear-growth) pattern as of July 2018, along with a list of
  the record-holders.
  Collisions within the pattern cause it to sprout its Nth
  [switch engine](/patterns/switch-engine) at generation T = ~224n-6.  The population of the
  pattern at time t is asymptotically proportional to t times log(t),
  so the growth rate is O(t ln(t)), faster than [linear growth](/patterns/linear-growth) but
  slower than [quadratic growth](/patterns/quadratic-growth).
