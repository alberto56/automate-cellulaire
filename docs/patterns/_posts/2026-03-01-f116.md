description: |
  An [elementary conduit](/patterns/elementary-conduit), one of the original sixteen
  [Herschel conduit](/patterns/herschel-conduit)s, discovered by Paul Callahan in February 1997.
  After 116 ticks, it produces a [Herschel](/patterns/herschel) at (32, 1) relative to the
  input.  Its [recovery time](/patterns/recovery-time) is 138 ticks; this can be reduced to 120
  ticks by adding extra mechanisms to suppress the internal glider.  It
  is [Spartan](/patterns/spartan) only if the following conduit is a [dependent conduit](/patterns/dependent-conduit),
  so that the [weld](/patterns/weld)ed [FNG](/patterns/fng) eater can be removed.  A [ghost Herschel](/patterns/ghost-herschel)
  in the pattern below marks the output location.
