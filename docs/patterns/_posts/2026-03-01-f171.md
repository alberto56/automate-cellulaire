  An [elementary conduit](/patterns/elementary-conduit), the seventeenth [Herschel conduit](/patterns/herschel-conduit),
  discovered by Brice Due in August 2006 in a search using only
  [eater](/patterns/eater)s as [catalyst](/patterns/catalyst)s.  This was the first new Herschel conduit
  discovery since 1998.  After 171 ticks, it produces a [Herschel](/patterns/herschel) at
  (29, -17) relative to the input.  A [ghost Herschel](/patterns/ghost-herschel) in the pattern
  below marks the output location.

  description: |
    The conduit's [recovery time](/patterns/recovery-time) is 227 ticks, slower than many of the
    original sixteen conduits because of the delayed destruction of a
    temporary blinker, though the circuit itself is clearly [Spartan](/patterns/spartan).
    The recovery time can be improved to 120 ticks by adding [sparker](/patterns/sparker)s
    of various periods to suppress the blinker.  See [clock](/patterns/clock) for a
    period-2 example.
    The central eater in the group of three to the northwest can be
    removed to release an additional [glider](/patterns/glider) output signal on a
    [transparent](/patterns/transparent) [lane](/patterns/lane).
