description: |
  Any pattern covering the whole plane that is periodic in both
  space and time.  The simplest (nonempty) agar is the [stable](/patterns/stable) one
  extended by the known [spacefiller](/patterns/spacefiller)s.  For some more examples see
  [chicken wire](/patterns/chicken-wire), [houndstooth agar](/patterns/houndstooth-agar), [onion rings](/patterns/onion-rings), [squaredance](/patterns/squaredance) and
  [Venetian blinds](/patterns/venetian-blinds).  Tiling the plane with the pattern O    - "......"O
  produces another interesting example: a p6 agar which has a phase of
  [density](/patterns/density) 3/4, which is the highest yet obtained for any phase of an
  oscillating pattern.  See [lone dot agar](/patterns/lone-dot-agar) for an agar composed of
  isolated cells.
