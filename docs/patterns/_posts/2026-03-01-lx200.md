description: |
  A [composite conduit](/patterns/composite-conduit), one of the original sixteen
  [Herschel conduit](/patterns/herschel-conduit)s, discovered by Paul Callahan in June 1997.  It is
  made up of two [elementary conduit](/patterns/elementary-conduit)s, HL141B + [BFx59H](/patterns/bfx59h). The Lx200
  and [F166](/patterns/f166) conduits are the two original [dependent conduit](/patterns/dependent-conduit)s
  (several more have since been discovered.)  After 200 ticks, it
  produces an inverted [Herschel](/patterns/herschel) turned 90 degrees counterclockwise at
  (17, -40) relative to the input.  Its [recovery time](/patterns/recovery-time) is 90 ticks.
  It can be made [Spartan](/patterns/spartan) by replacing the [snake](/patterns/snake)s with [eater1](/patterns/eater1)s in
  one of two orientations. A [ghost Herschel](/patterns/ghost-herschel) in the pattern below
  marks the output location.
