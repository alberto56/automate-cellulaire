description: |
  A [composite conduit](/patterns/composite-conduit), one of the original sixteen
  [Herschel conduit](/patterns/herschel-conduit)s, discovered by Dave Buckingham in August 1996.
  It is made up of three [elementary conduit](/patterns/elementary-conduit)s, HLx69R + [RF28B](/patterns/rf28b) +
  [BFx59H](/patterns/bfx59h).  After 156 ticks, it produces a [Herschel](/patterns/herschel) turned 90
  degrees counterclockwise at (17, -41) relative to the input.  Its
  [recovery time](/patterns/recovery-time) is 62 ticks.  It can be made [Spartan](/patterns/spartan) by replacing
  the [snake](/patterns/snake) with an [eater1](/patterns/eater1) in one of two orientations.  Additional
  gliders can be produced by removing the southeasternmost eater, or by
  replacing the RF28B elementary conduit by an alternate version.  A
  [ghost Herschel](/patterns/ghost-herschel) in the pattern below marks the output location.
