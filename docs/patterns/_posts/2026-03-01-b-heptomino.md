description: |
  (stabilizes at time 148)  This is a very common
  [methuselah](/patterns/methuselah) that evolves into three [block](/patterns/block)s, two [glider](/patterns/glider)s and a
  [ship](/patterns/ship) after 148 generations. Compare with [Herschel](/patterns/herschel), which appears
  at generation 20 of the B-heptomino's evolution. B-heptominoes
  acquired particular importance in 1996 due to Dave Buckingham's work
  on [B track](/patterns/b-track)s.  See in particular
  [My Experience with B-heptominos in Oscillators](/patterns/my-experience-with-b-heptominos-in-oscillators). This pattern often arises with the cell at top left shifted one
  space to the left, producing a seven-bit [polyplet](/patterns/polyplet) that shares the
  same eight-bit descendant but is not technically a heptomino at all.
  This alternate form is shown as the input for [elementary](/patterns/elementary)
  [converter](/patterns/converter) patterns such as [BFx59H](/patterns/bfx59h) and [BRx46B](/patterns/brx46b). This is standard
  practice for elementary [conduit](/patterns/conduit)s, since many of these conduits do
  in fact produce this alternate form as output.
  The B-heptomino is considered a failed [puffer](/patterns/puffer) or failed
  [spaceship](/patterns/spaceship), since on its own it travels at c/2 for only a short time
  before being affected by its own trailing debris.  However, it can be
  stabilized into a c/2 puffer or into a [clean](/patterns/clean) c/2 rake or spaceship.
  See, e.g., [ecologist](/patterns/ecologist).
