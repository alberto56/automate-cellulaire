description: |
  Any [oscillator](/patterns/oscillator) whose [rotor](/patterns/rotor) consists of a string of cells each of which is adjacent to exactly two other rotor cells, except for the endpoints which are adjacent to only one other rotor cell.  Compare [muttering moat](/patterns/muttering-moat).  Examples include the [beacon](/patterns/beacon), the [great on-off](/patterns/great-on-off), the [light bulb](/patterns/light-bulb) and the [spark coil](/patterns/spark-coil).  The following less trivial example (by Dean Hickerson, August 1997) is the only one known with more than four cells in its rotor.  It is p4 and has a 6-cell rotor.
