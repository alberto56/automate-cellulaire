description: |
  A [signal](/patterns/signal) [converter](/patterns/converter) that accepts a single input signal
  and produces two or more output signals, usually of the same type as
  the input.  An older term for this is [fanout](/patterns/fanout), or "fanout device".
  A sub-category is the [one-time](/patterns/one-time) splitter, which is not technically
  a converter because it can only be used once.  One-time splitters are
  usually small [constellation](/patterns/constellation)s that produce two or more [clean](/patterns/clean)
  gliders when struck by a single glider.  In other words, they are
  multi-glider [seed](/patterns/seed)s.  These are important for constructing
  self-destruct circuitry in [self-constructing](/patterns/self-constructing) spaceships.
  The following combination, a [syringe](/patterns/syringe) attached to an SE7T14
  [converter](/patterns/converter) combined with an [NW31](/patterns/nw31) converter, is one of the smallest
  known glider splitters as of July 2018.  Another small splitter with
  a 90-degree [colour-changing](/patterns/colour-changing) output is shown under [reflector](/patterns/reflector).
