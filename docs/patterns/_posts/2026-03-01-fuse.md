description: |
  A [wick](/patterns/wick) [burn](/patterns/burn)ing at one end.  For examples, see [baker](/patterns/baker),
  [beacon maker](/patterns/beacon-maker), [blinker ship](/patterns/blinker-ship), [boat maker](/patterns/boat-maker), [cow](/patterns/cow), [harvester](/patterns/harvester),
  [lightspeed wire](/patterns/lightspeed-wire), [pi ship](/patterns/pi-ship), [reverse fuse](/patterns/reverse-fuse), [superstring](/patterns/superstring) and
  [washerwoman](/patterns/washerwoman).  Useful fuses are usually [clean](/patterns/clean), but see also
  [reburnable fuse](/patterns/reburnable-fuse).
  A fuse can [burn](/patterns/burn) arbitrarily slowly, as demonstrated by the
  example [Blockic](/patterns/blockic) fuse below.  A [signal](/patterns/signal), alternating between
  [glider](/patterns/glider) and [MWSS](/patterns/mwSs) form, travels up and down between two rows of
  blocks in a series of [one-time](/patterns/one-time) [turner](/patterns/turner) reactions.  The spacing
  shown here causes the fuse to burn 24 cells to the right every 240
  generations, for a speed of c/10.  Moving the bottom half further
  from the top half by any even number of cells will slow down the
  burning even further.
