description: |
  A pattern that dies.  The term is typically used to describe a
  collection of cells periodically thrown off by an [oscillator](/patterns/oscillator) or
  [spaceship](/patterns/spaceship), but other dying patterns, particularly those consisting
  or only one or two cells (such as produced by certain glider
  collisions, for example), are also described as sparks.  For examples
  of small sparks see [unix](/patterns/unix) and [HWSS](/patterns/hwSs).  Examples of much larger
  sparks are seen in [Schick engine](/patterns/schick-engine) and [twin bees shuttle spark](/patterns/twin-bees-shuttle-spark).
