description: |
  Movement of information through the Life universe.  Signals
  can be carried by [spaceship](/patterns/spaceship)s, [fuse](/patterns/fuse)s, [drifter](/patterns/drifter)s, or [conduit](/patterns/conduit)s.
  Spaceships can only transfer a signal at the speed of the spaceship,
  while fuses can transfer a signal at speeds up to the
  [speed of light](/patterns/speed-of-light).
  In practice, many signals are encoded as the presence or absence of
  a [glider](/patterns/glider) or other spaceship at a particular point at a particular
  time.  Such signals can be combined by the collision of gliders to
  form logic operations such as AND, OR, and NOT gates. Signals can be
  duplicated using [glider duplicator](/patterns/glider-duplicator)s or other [fanout](/patterns/fanout) devices, and
  can be used up by causing [perturbation](/patterns/perturbation)s on other parts of the Life
  object.
  Signals are used in [Herschel conduit](/patterns/herschel-conduit) circuitry,
  [universal constructor](/patterns/universal-constructor)s, [macro-spaceship](/patterns/macro-spaceship)s, and other computational
  patterns such as the [pi calculator](/patterns/pi-calculator) and [Osqrtlogt](/patterns/osqrtlogt) patterns.
