description: |
  An [elementary conduit](/patterns/elementary-conduit), one of the original sixteen
  [Herschel conduit](/patterns/herschel-conduit)s, discovered by Dave Buckingham in August 1996.
  After 77 ticks, it produces an inverted [Herschel](/patterns/herschel) at (25, -8)
  relative to the input.  Its [recovery time](/patterns/recovery-time) is 61 ticks; this can be
  reduced slightly by suppressing the output Herschel's glider, as in
  the [L112](/patterns/l112) case.  A [pipsquirter](/patterns/pipsquirter) can replace the blinker-suppressing
  eater to produce an extra glider output.  It is one of the simplest
  known [Spartan](/patterns/spartan) conduits, and one of the few [elementary conduit](/patterns/elementary-conduit)s in
  the original set of sixteen.
  In January 2016, Tanner Jacobi discovered a [Spartan](/patterns/spartan) method of
  extracting an extra glider output (top variant below).  A
  [ghost Herschel](/patterns/ghost-herschel) marks the output location for each variant.
