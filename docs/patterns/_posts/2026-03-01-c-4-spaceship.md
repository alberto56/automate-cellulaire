description: |
  A [spaceship](/patterns/spaceship) travelling at one quarter of the
  [speed of light](/patterns/speed-of-light).  The first such spaceship to be discovered was, of
  course, the [glider](/patterns/glider), and this remained the only known example until
  December 1989, when Dean Hickerson found the first orthogonal
  example, [119P4H1V0](/patterns/119p4h1v0), and also a new diagonal example (the
  [big glider](/patterns/big-glider)). For other examples see [B29](/patterns/b29), [Canada goose](/patterns/canada-goose), [crane](/patterns/crane),
  [Enterprise](/patterns/enterprise), [edge-repair spaceship](/patterns/edge-repair-spaceship) (third pattern),
  [non-monotonic](/patterns/non-monotonic), [Orion](/patterns/orion), [quarter](/patterns/quarter), [sparky](/patterns/sparky), [swan](/patterns/swan) and [tagalong](/patterns/tagalong).
  It is known that c/4 is the fastest possible speed for a (45-degree)
  diagonal spaceship.
