description: "Run-length encoded.  Run-length encoding is a simple (but not\nvery
  efficient) method of file compression.  In Life the term refers\nto a specific ASCII
  encoding used for patterns in Conway's Life and\nother similar cellular automata.
  \ This encoding was introduced by\nDave Buckingham and is now the usual means of
  exchanging relatively\nsmall patterns by email or in online forum discussions.\nAs
  an example of the rle format, here is a representation of the\n[Gosper glider gun](/patterns/gosper-glider-gun).
  \ The \"run lengths\" are the numbers, b's are dead\ncells, o's are live cells,
  and dollar signs signal new lines: x = 36, y = 9, rule = B3/S23  \t  24bo$22bobo$12boo6boo12boo$11bo3bo4boo12boo$oo8bo
  5bo3boo$oo8bo3boboo4bobo$10bo5bo7bo$11bo3bo$12boo! Over the years RLE format has
  been extended to handle patterns with    multiple states, neighborhoods, rules,
  and universe sizes.  A completely different encoding, [macrocell](/patterns/macrocell)
  format, is used for\nrepetitive patterns that may have very large [population](/patterns/population)s.\n"
