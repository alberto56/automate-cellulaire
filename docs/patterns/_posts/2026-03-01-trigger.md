description: |
  A [signal](/patterns/signal), usually a single [glider](/patterns/glider), that collides with a
  [seed](/patterns/seed) [constellation](/patterns/constellation) to produce a relatively rare still life or
  oscillator, or an output [spaceship](/patterns/spaceship) or other signal.  The
  constellation is destroyed or damaged in the process; compare
  [circuit](/patterns/circuit), [reflector](/patterns/reflector).  Here a pair of trigger gliders strike a
  [dirty](/patterns/dirty) seed constellation assembled by Chris Cain in March 2015, to
  launch a three-engine [Cordership](/patterns/cordership).

  description: |
    "Trigger" is also used when a spaceship reacts with another object
    to cause a reaction to occur whenever desired (but perhaps only at
    particular intervals).  The object being triggered lies [dormant](/patterns/dormant)
    until the reaction is required.  All [turner](/patterns/turner)s and [freeze-dried](/patterns/freeze-dried)
    constellations are triggerable.
    In some cases the object is not destroyed so that the reaction can
    be repeated after some [repeat time](/patterns/repeat-time).  See for example [converter](/patterns/converter)
    and [reflector](/patterns/reflector), and more specifically [MWSS out of the blue](/patterns/mwSs-out-of-the-blue) and
    [queen bee shuttle pair](/patterns/queen-bee-shuttle-pair).
