description: |
  (p960, p30 circuitry)  A variant of the
  [telegraph](/patterns/telegraph) constructed by Louis-Francois Handfield in February 2017,
  using periodic components to achieve a transmission rate of one bit
  per 192 ticks.  The same ten signals are sent as in the original
  [telegraph](/patterns/telegraph) and the [p1 telegraph](/patterns/p1-telegraph), but information is encoded more
  efficiently in the timing of those signals.  Specifically, the new
  transmitter sends five bits every 960 ticks by adjusting the relative
  timings inside each of the five mirror-image paired subunits of the
  composite signal in the beehive-chain [lightspeed wire](/patterns/lightspeed-wire) [fuse](/patterns/fuse).
