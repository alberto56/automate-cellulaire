description: |
  Any [oscillator](/patterns/oscillator) with a row of dead cells down the middle
  and whose two halves are mirror images of one another, both halves
  being required for the oscillator to work.  The classic examples are
  the [pulsar](/patterns/pulsar) and the [tumbler](/patterns/tumbler).  If still lifes are considered as p1
  oscillators then there are numerous simple examples that include this
  kind of central [gutter](/patterns/gutter), such as [table on table](/patterns/table-on-table), [dead spark coil](/patterns/dead-spark-coil)
  and [cis-mirrored R-bee](/patterns/cis-mirrored-r-bee). Some spaceships, such as the [brain](/patterns/brain), the
  [snail](/patterns/snail) and the [spider](/patterns/spider), use the same principle.
