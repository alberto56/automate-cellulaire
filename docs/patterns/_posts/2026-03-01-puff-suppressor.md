description: |
  An attachment at the back of a [line puffer](/patterns/line-puffer) that
  suppresses all or some of its puffing action.  The example below (by
  Hartmut Holzwart) has a 3-cell puff suppressor at the back which
  suppresses the entire puff, making a p2 [spaceship](/patterns/spaceship).  If you delete
  this puff suppressor then you get a p60 double [beehive](/patterns/beehive) [puffer](/patterns/puffer).
  Puff suppressors were first recognised by Alan Hensel in April 1994.
