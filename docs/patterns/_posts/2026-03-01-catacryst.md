description: |
  A 58-cell [quadratic growth](/patterns/quadratic-growth) pattern found by Nick Gotts in
  April 2000.  This was formerly the smallest such pattern known, but
  has since been superseded by the related [metacatacryst](/patterns/metacatacryst).  See
  [switch-engine ping-pong](/patterns/switch-engine-ping-pong) for the lowest-population
  [superlinear growth](/patterns/superlinear-growth) pattern as of July 2018, along with a list of
  the record-holders.
  The catacryst consists of three [ark](/patterns/ark)s plus a glider-producing
  [switch engine](/patterns/switch-engine).  It produces a block-laying switch engine every
  47616 generations.  Each block-laying switch engine has only a finite
  life, but the length of this life increases linearly with each new
  switch engine, so that the pattern overall grows quadratically, as an
  unusual type of MMS [breeder](/patterns/breeder).
