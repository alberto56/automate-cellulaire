description: |
  The collection of [cell](/patterns/cell)s that are alive during
  some part of a given active reaction.  This term is used for
  [Herschel](/patterns/herschel) [circuit](/patterns/circuit)s and other stable circuitry, whereas
  [construction envelope](/patterns/construction-envelope) is specific to recipes in [self-constructing](/patterns/self-constructing)
  circuitry.
  There are some subtleties at the edges of the envelope.
  Specifically, two reactions that have the exact same set of cells
  defining their envelopes may have different behavior when placed next
  to a single-cell protrusion like the tail of an [eater1](/patterns/eater1), or one side
  of a [tub](/patterns/tub).  The difference depends on whether two orthogonally
  adjacent cells at the edge of the envelope are ever simultaneously
  alive, within the protruding cell's [zone of influence](/patterns/zone-of-influence).
