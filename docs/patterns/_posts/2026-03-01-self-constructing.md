description: |
  A type of pattern, generally a [macro-spaceship](/patterns/macro-spaceship),
  that contains encoded construction information about itself, and
  makes a complete copy of itself using those instructions.  The
  [Gemini](/patterns/gemini), [linear propagator](/patterns/linear-propagator), [spiral growth](/patterns/spiral-growth) patterns, [Demonoid](/patterns/demonoid)s
  and [Orthogonoid](/patterns/orthogonoid) are examples of self-constructing patterns.
  Self-constructing spaceships often have trivially adjustable speeds.
  In many cases, the direction of travel can also be altered, less
  easily, by changing the encoded [construction recipe](/patterns/construction-recipe).  Compare
  [self-supporting](/patterns/self-supporting), [elementary](/patterns/elementary).
