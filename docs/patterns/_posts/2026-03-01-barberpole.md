description: |
  Any p2 oscillator in the infinite sequence [bipole](/patterns/bipole),
  [tripole](/patterns/tripole), [quadpole](/patterns/quadpole), [pentapole](/patterns/pentapole), [hexapole](/patterns/hexapole), [heptapole](/patterns/heptapole)     - "..." (It
  wasn't my idea to suddenly change from Latin to Greek.) This sequence
  of oscillators was found by the MIT group in 1970. The term is also
  used (usually in the form "barber pole") to describe other
  [extensible](/patterns/extensible) sections of oscillators or spaceships, especially those
  (usually of period 2) in which all generations look alike except for
  a translation and/or rotation/reflection.  Any barberpole can be
  lengthened by the reaction shown in [barbershop](/patterns/barbershop).  See also
  [pseudo-barberpole](/patterns/pseudo-barberpole).
